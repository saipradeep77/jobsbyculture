Culture Overview

Built by the people who created Spark

Databricks was founded in 2013 by the original creators of Apache Spark, Delta Lake, and MLflow. That engineering-first DNA runs deep: the company still operates with the conviction that the best ideas should come from the people closest to the technology.

With a $62B valuation and roughly 7,000 employees, Databricks has grown into the undisputed leader of the data lakehouse category. The culture balances startup intensity with the resources of a late-stage giant — internal tech talks, generous learning budgets, and a genuine belief that engineers should drive product decisions.

Compensation and equity are consistently cited as top-tier, and the company has maintained a strong 82% employee recommendation rate. CEO approval sits at 88%, reflecting leadership that most employees trust.

That said, hypergrowth has its tradeoffs. Org restructures are frequent, some legacy Spark-era tech debt persists, and work-life balance varies significantly by team. Large-company politics are starting to appear. But for engineers who want to work on category-defining infrastructure at massive scale, Databricks remains one of the strongest choices in the industry.
